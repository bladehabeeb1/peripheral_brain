Nitrogen balance is a measurment of the net change in nitrogen in an individuals body. In a healthy individual, the nitrogen balance should be approximatly 0. In a catabolic individual (patients suffering from sepsis, burns, majory surgery, etc) will exhibit a negative nitrogen balance, which is not ideal as this means they are breaking down protein at a higher rate than they are producing it. This lost nitrogen must be repleated to allow the individual to enter an anabolic state and repair cellular damage. A goal nitrogen balance for a hospitalized patient is (+)3-(+)5.

Enteral Nutrition

Short term EN is indicated in patients who will need EN for less than 3 weeks, and who have trouble swallowing, head or neck surgery, trauma or burns, esophageal obstruction, dysphagia, or any condition preventing normal eating. Options for short term EN administration include ND, NJ, and NG tubes (and their oral variations).

Long term EN is indicated for the same reasons as short term for periods of time longer than 3 weeks. Options for long term EN include jejunostomy and gastrostomy.
